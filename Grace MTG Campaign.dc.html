The Great Gold Rip Off

Here’s how they overcharge the people who trust them.

It starts by getting you off regular gold coins. Regular coins sell for close to the going price of gold — a price you can check on your phone. So a company that wants to charge you more sells you a different kind of coin, one where the real price is hard to find.

Regular gold coins

Close to the going rate for gold. Easy to check, easy to sell back for what they’re worth.

“Special” coins

Cost more than regular coins, for reasons that are hard to pin down.

“Rare” coins

Sold as collector’s items for far more than the gold in them. Hard to sell later for anything close.

They talk these coins up with the same few lines every time: “limited supply,” “private,” “the government can’t take it,” “it protects you if the market falls.” The words sound good — but the coin costs far more than the gold in it, and you don’t find out until you try to sell.
