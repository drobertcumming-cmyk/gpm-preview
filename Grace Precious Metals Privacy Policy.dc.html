Grace Precious Metals (“Grace Precious Metals,” “Grace,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use and share it, the choices you have, and the rights available to you under applicable privacy laws.

This Policy applies to information we collect through our website at gracepreciousmetals.com (the “Website”), through telephone calls and text messages between you and us, through the forms and requests you submit to us, and through your interactions with our advertising and communications. It does not apply to the practices of third parties we do not own or control.

By using the Website or providing us with your information, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy. If you do not agree with it, please do not use the Website or submit information to us.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information in three ways: information you provide to us directly, information we collect automatically, and information we receive from third parties.

Information you provide to us. When you request information about a Gold IRA or precious metals, request a guide, book a call, open an account, or otherwise contact us, you may provide:

  • Identifiers such as your name, email address, mailing address, and telephone number.
  • Demographic information such as age range or approximate retirement timeline, where you choose to share it.
  • Financial and account information necessary to open or fund a precious metals IRA or complete a purchase, which may include information about existing retirement accounts, funding amounts, and, where required to process a transaction, government identifiers and financial account details.
  • The contents of your communications with us, including form submissions, emails, and notes from telephone conversations.

Information we collect automatically. When you visit the Website, we and our service providers may automatically collect:

  • Device and connection information such as IP address, browser type, operating system, and referring pages.
  • Usage information such as the pages you view, links you click, and the dates and times of your visits.
  • Information collected through cookies, pixels, and similar technologies (see Section 5, Cookies and Tracking Technologies).

Information from third parties. We may receive information about you from marketing and advertising partners, publishers and affiliates who refer you to us, lead-generation providers, and analytics providers, consistent with those parties’ own privacy practices and any consent you gave them.

Call recording. Telephone calls with our advisors may be monitored or recorded for quality, training, and compliance purposes. Where required by law, we will notify you at the start of the call.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Respond to your inquiries and provide the guides, information, and services you request.
  • Establish, fund, and service precious metals IRA accounts and process transactions.
  • Communicate with you about your account, your requests, and administrative matters.
  • Send you marketing and educational communications about our products and services, subject to your consent and your right to opt out (see Section 6, Your Choices).
  • Operate, maintain, analyze, and improve the Website and our services.
  • Personalize your experience and measure the effectiveness of our advertising.
  • Detect, prevent, and address fraud, security issues, and misuse.
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, tax, audit, and recordkeeping obligations, including those applicable to precious metals dealers and IRA-related services.
  • Establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

We do not use or disclose sensitive personal information for purposes other than those permitted under applicable law.

3. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your sensitive personal information, and we do not share your telephone opt-in or text-messaging consent data with any third party for that third party’s own marketing purposes (see Section 4, Text Messages and Telephone Calls). Subject to that, we may share personal information with:

  • Service providers who perform functions on our behalf, such as account custodians and depositories, payment processors, hosting and IT providers, CRM and communications platforms, analytics providers, and professional advisors. These parties are permitted to use your information only to provide services to us.
  • Advertising and analytics partners. We may share certain online identifiers and usage information with advertising and analytics partners to measure and deliver advertising. Depending on the tools we use, this activity may be considered “sharing” for targeted advertising or a “sale” under certain state privacy laws. You can exercise choices about this as described in Sections 6 and 7.
  • Referral, publisher, and affiliate partners where you were referred to us or where we coordinate a promotion, limited to what is necessary for that purpose.
  • Legal and safety recipients, including regulators, law enforcement, and other parties where we believe disclosure is necessary to comply with law, enforce our agreements, or protect the rights, property, or safety of Grace Precious Metals, our customers, or others.
  • Business transfers. If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to this Policy.

4. Text Messages and Telephone Calls

If you provide your telephone number and consent to receive them, we may contact you by telephone call and by text (SMS) message, including through automated dialing technology, for the purposes you agreed to, such as responding to your request, servicing your account, and marketing. Consent to receive marketing calls or texts is not a condition of purchasing any product or service. Message and data rates may apply, and message frequency varies.

Revoking consent. You may revoke your consent to receive marketing calls or text messages at any time by replying STOP to any text message, by contacting us at privacy@gracepreciousmetals.com or (562) 473-1726, or by any other reasonable means. After you opt out, we may send a single confirmation message, and we may still contact you for non-marketing purposes such as servicing an existing account or as otherwise permitted by law.

Protection of your consent data. Information collected as part of our text-messaging program — including your mobile opt-in and consent — will not be shared with or sold to any third party for that third party’s own marketing purposes under any circumstances.

We honor applicable federal and state do-not-call and telemarketing rules. If you wish to be added to our internal do-not-call list, contact us using the details in Section 12.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We and our service providers use cookies, web beacons, pixels, and similar technologies to operate the Website, remember your preferences, understand how the Website is used, and support advertising and measurement.

The specific tools we use may include analytics and advertising technologies. To complete before publishing — list the tools Grace actually deploys (e.g. Google Analytics, Google Ads, Meta Pixel). These providers may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites.

You can control cookies through your browser settings, and you may be able to opt out of certain advertising cookies through industry tools such as the Digital Advertising Alliance (optout.aboutads.info) and the Network Advertising Initiative (optout.networkadvertising.org). We honor Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where required by law. Because there is no common industry standard, we do not currently respond to other “Do Not Track” browser signals.

6. Your Choices

  • Marketing emails. You can unsubscribe from marketing emails using the link in any such email. We may still send you non-marketing messages about your account or requests.
  • Calls and texts. You can opt out as described in Section 4.
  • Cookies and advertising. You can manage cookies and advertising choices as described in Section 5.
  • Updating your information. You can ask us to update or correct your information by contacting us using the details in Section 12.

7. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • The right to know or access the personal information we hold about you and how we use and share it.
  • The right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information.
  • The right to data portability, where applicable.
  • The right to opt out of the “sale” or “sharing” of your personal information and of targeted advertising.
  • The right to limit the use of sensitive personal information, where applicable.
  • The right not to be discriminated against for exercising your privacy rights.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@gracepreciousmetals.com or by mail at the address in Section 12. We will take reasonable steps to verify your identity before responding, and we may be unable to fulfill a request if we cannot verify it. You may use an authorized agent to submit a request on your behalf, subject to verification. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law. If we deny your request, you may appeal by contacting us at the same address.

California Residents

If you are a California resident, you have the rights described above under the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended (the CCPA/CPRA), including the right to know, delete, and correct your personal information, the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information, and the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information.

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information. To opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, contact us at privacy@gracepreciousmetals.com or enable the Global Privacy Control signal in your browser. As stated in Section 4, we never share or sell text-messaging opt-in or consent data.

Shine the Light. California Civil Code Section 1798.83 permits California residents to request certain information about our disclosure of personal information to third parties for their own direct marketing purposes. To make such a request, contact us at privacy@gracepreciousmetals.com.

Notice of financial incentive / non-discrimination. We will not deny you goods or services, charge you different prices, or provide a different level of quality because you exercised your privacy rights, except where permitted by law.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy, to provide our services, to comply with our legal, tax, and regulatory obligations (which, for financial and IRA-related records, may require retention for a number of years), to resolve disputes, and to enforce our agreements. When information is no longer needed, we take reasonable steps to delete or de-identify it.

9. Security

We use commercially reasonable physical, technical, and administrative safeguards designed to protect personal information against loss, misuse, and unauthorized access, disclosure, or alteration. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, however, and we cannot guarantee the absolute security of your information. Any transmission of personal information to us is at your own risk. If we become aware of a security incident affecting your personal information, we will notify you and any regulators as required by applicable law.

10. Third-Party Websites and Links

The Website may contain links to third-party websites, tools, or services that we do not control, including those of custodians, depositories, publishers, and advertising partners. This Policy does not apply to those third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third party before providing your information to them.

11. Children’s Privacy

The Website and our services are intended for adults and are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under the age of 18. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it.
