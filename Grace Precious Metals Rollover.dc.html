Gold IRA / How it works

How it works

A Gold IRA lets you hold physical gold inside a retirement account that follows the same IRS rules as any other IRA — same contribution limits, same tax treatment, same required minimum distributions. The only thing that changes is what you own and where it sits.

01 — What it is

A retirement account that holds metal, not paper.

The structure was created by the Taxpayer Relief Act of 1997, which expanded Internal Revenue Code Section 408(m) to let IRAs hold specific forms of physical gold, silver, platinum, and palladium. Before that, IRAs could hold paper assets only.

Tax treatment follows the same rules as any other IRA. Traditional contributions are pre-tax and taxed on distribution; Roth contributions are post-tax and not taxed on qualified distribution. Required minimum distributions begin at age 73.

The statute
IRC §408(m), expanded by the Taxpayer Relief Act of 1997.
Same rules as any IRA
Contribution limits, tax treatment, RMDs at 73.
The only difference
What the account holds — physical bullion instead of paper.

A Gold IRA has three roles, and they are deliberately separate — no single party holds your money, your metal, and the paperwork at once.

02 — How you fund it

You roll over from an account you already have.

The funds move directly from your existing account to your new IRA’s custodian. You never receive the money. There is no tax event. There is no penalty.

Two cases differ slightly: an inherited IRA follows the SECURE Act’s distribution rules, and a transfer from another Gold IRA company can move metal in kind rather than converting to cash and back.
