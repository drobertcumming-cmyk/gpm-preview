Let me show you how it's done

First, they get you off regular gold coins.

Regular gold coins sell for close to the going price of gold — a price you can look up on your phone in seconds. That's a problem for a company that wants to charge you more. So they sell you a different kind of coin, one where the real price is hard to find.

Regular gold coins

Priced close to the going rate for gold. Easy to check. Easy to sell back for close to what they're worth.

"Special" coins

Cost more than regular coins, for reasons that are hard to pin down.

"Rare" coins

Sold as collector's items for far more than the gold inside them. Hard to sell later for anything close.

They talk these coins up with the same few lines every time: "limited supply," "private," "the government can't take it," "it protects you if the market falls." I heard them for years. The words sound good — but the coin costs far more than the gold in it, and you don't find out until you try to sell.

Why it keeps happening

Four reasons they get away with it.

Every one of them works against you before you've said a word.

so you can't compare it to anything before you're on the phone.

02

these special coins aren't sold out in the open, so there's nothing to compare them to.

03

Nobody's really watching

there are very few rules covering how these coins are sold.

04

The salesman gets paid more

the more you spend, the bigger his cut. He's not really on your side.

Here's what it costs you

On $100,000, you can lose $17,000 to $33,000 — and in the worst cases, far more.

Once you add up everything you pay to buy and everything you lose to sell, the true cost for most companies runs between 17 and 33 cents on every dollar. That's what's typical. In the worst cases regulators have found, people were charged up to 130% more than their gold was worth — more than double. On $100,000, that isn't $30,000 gone. It's far more.
