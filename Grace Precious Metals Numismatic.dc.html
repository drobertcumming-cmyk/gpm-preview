Pricing / Numismatic Coins

Product posture

We do not sell what this industry steers you into.

Numismatic, “premium,” “exclusive,” “rare,” and “proof” coins are the single most documented source of consumer harm in Gold IRA enforcement records. Our catalogue does not include any of them — and this page explains why that refusal is a product-design decision, not a marketing position.

We do not sell

×Numismatic coins
×“Premium” coins
×“Exclusive” coins
×Proof sets
Standard IRS-eligible bullion only.

What the terms mean

Three categories. Two legitimate. One the pattern.

The markups below describe what dealers charge above the underlying metal value. Tier 1 is bounded; Tier 2 is legitimate but specialist; Tier 3 is where the documented harm sits.

Tier 1 Bounded
6–15%
markup over spot

Standard bullion

Coins and bars whose value is the underlying weight of metal at market prices — Eagles, Maple Leafs, Philharmonics, LBMA bars. The premium reflects minting cost and dealer margin: bounded, transparent, verifiable.

Tier 2 Specialist
15–30%+
markup over spot

Genuine numismatic

Coins valued for rarity, history, condition, or collector interest rather than weight. A legitimate segment served by specialist dealers, auctions, and grading services. Value is set by scarcity and provenance — not a label.

Tier 3 The harm sits here
50–100%+
markup over spot

“Premium” in the IRA channel

Retail Gold IRA products positioned as carrying value above metal content. Some carry genuine numismatic value; others carry a marketing label. The gap between marketed value and independent resale value is where the pattern of harm sits.

The documented pattern

The pattern, as enforcement records describe it.

Between 2015 and 2024, regulators brought actions against dealers whose model centered on selling numismatic and “premium” coins to retirement holders at markups that bore no relationship to independent resale value. The pattern is consistent: the product’s resale value, at the time of purchase, is materially below the price paid.

Most customers discovered the gap only when attempting to liquidate — at which point the buyback offer confirmed what the markup had already determined.

Why the catalogue excludes them

A product-design decision, not a marketing position.

Every product on our catalogue has a transparent, globally quoted spot price you can verify at any time. Excluding the category most associated with documented harm removes the incentive, the information asymmetry, and the buyback leverage that make the pattern possible. You cannot be steered into a product that does not exist on the catalogue.

It also means Grace forgoes the highest-margin category in the channel. That trade-off is intentional: a lower margin per customer, and a catalogue that can be defended in plain language.
